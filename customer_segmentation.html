Segmenting Value, Risk, and Growth at Infinity Bank

Customer Retention | Risk Management | Customer Segmentation

Customer Segmentation for Profitability in Banking
Shruti Deshpande

Business Challenge & Segmentation Objective

Infinity Bank faced a familiar but high-impact problem: not all customers contribute equally to profitability, and treating them the same is expensive. Marketing, risk, and retention teams needed a clearer way to decide who to invest in, who to protect, and where to pull back.


As part of Customer Analytics project, we focused on building a customer segmentation framework that connects data directly to those decisions — identifying customers to prioritize for cross-selling, retain proactively, or manage for risk. This project used customer segmentation and predictive analytics to turn a 2,205-customer weighted sample into clear, decision-ready segments aligned to business actions.


Segmentation & Modeling Approach

We segmented customers based on product depth and financial behavior across three core products: Current Accounts, Credit Cards, and Mortgages. The dataset included 1,000 customers per product-combination segment, allowing consistent comparison across customer types.

To move from descriptive segmentation to decision-ready insights, we built logistic regression models, each aligned to a specific business question:

  • Who is most likely to take a credit card?
  • Who is at elevated risk of default?
  • Which customers are profitable — and which are likely to churn?

Rather than using raw predictions, customers were grouped into high, medium, and low probability segments, ensuring outputs could be directly used by marketing, risk, and collection teams.


What the Segments Revealed

Cross-Sell Opportunity
The credit card propensity model surfaced a distinct high-propensity segment of 239 customers representing the strongest immediate growth opportunity. These customers showed strong current account balances and revenues, making them ideal targets for focused cross-sell campaigns. Medium-propensity customers could be reached through low-cost channels, while low-propensity customers were intentionally deprioritized to avoid inefficient spending.

Risk Exposure
Default risk modeling identified a small but critical subset of 52 customers with significantly higher probabilities of default. Isolating this segment enables earlier intervention and more efficient use of risk management resources, without over-monitoring the broader customer base.

Profitability & Churn Dynamics
Profitability analysis showed that credit card revenues and service costs were the primary drivers of customer value. Several commonly assumed predictors, including certain balance and mortgage-related variables, were not statistically significant. Churn risk varied meaningfully by product depth, reinforcing the importance of multi-product relationships in long-term retention.

Business Value Delivered

This segmentation-driven approach allowed Infinity Bank to move from broad, reactive strategies to focused, data-backed decision-making:

  • Focus cross-sell efforts on 239 high-propensity customers with the highest expected return
  • Proactively manage risk by prioritizing 52 high-risk customers for monitoring and intervention
  • Align retention and marketing resources with customers who actually drive profit, rather than relying on surface-level metrics

The result was a clear prioritization framework that leadership could operationalize across teams, balancing growth, risk, and profitability.


Limitations & Next Iteration

The analysis was limited by static, point-in-time data and incomplete customer history. Future iterations would strengthen the framework by:

  • Introducing time-series analysis to capture behavioral change
  • Tracking customer movement across product combinations
  • Validating cross-sell and retention strategies through A/B testing
  • Enriching the dataset with additional behavioral and demographic features

Even with these constraints, the project demonstrates how customer segmentation, when paired with predictive modeling, can directly shape business strategy.
